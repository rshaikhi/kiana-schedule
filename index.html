The one thing to get right each month

  • 11mo: keep iron-rich food in every meal — the biggest nutritional risk this year
  • 12mo: don't overshoot milk volume once she's on whole milk — it quietly displaces the iron she needs
  • 13-14mo: get the bottle fully retired — the longer it lingers, the harder it is to drop

Also worth knowing

  • Appetite may visibly drop after 12 months — growth velocity slows a lot after the first birthday. Less eating isn't a red flag on its own.
  • Choking hazards don't end at 12 months — whole grapes, nuts, popcorn, hard candy stay off-limits to age 4.
  • Messy self-feeding is progress, not regression — it's how pincer grasp and utensil skill build.

Ask at the 12-month well-visit

  • Vitamin D: once formula stops, does she need a supplement drop? (Common in Canada given lower sun exposure — depends on how much fortified milk she actually drinks.)
  • Iron / anemia screen: many pediatricians check hemoglobin around 12 months, especially for formula-fed babies transitioning off fortified milk — worth confirming it's on the visit checklist.
  • Fluoride: teeth are erupting — ask whether varnish or supplementation is recommended.

12 Months - The Milk Switch

🥑 12 Months — The Milk Switch

Aug 2026

Formula → whole milk, phased over ~1 week · Target 16-20oz/day, hard cap 24oz · Bottle → cup weaning starts · Honey now allowed

Formula → Whole Milk, Day by Day

A gradual swap avoids a flat-out refusal — formula and milk taste different. Do this in her cup, not the bottle, where possible.

DaysMix
Day 1-23/4 formula + 1/4 whole milk
Day 3-4Half formula + half whole milk
Day 5-61/4 formula + 3/4 whole milk
Day 7+Full whole milk (pasteurized, homogenized — not skim/2% before age 2)

⚠️ Don't overshoot milk volume — "milk anemia" is real

Target 16-20oz/day. Never exceed 24oz/day.

Whole milk has essentially no iron, and too much of it does two things at once: it fills her up so she eats less iron-rich food, and heavy cow's-milk intake can itself cause small amounts of GI blood loss in toddlers. This combination is the single most common cause of iron-deficiency anemia at this age. If she seems to want more milk than that, offer water or food instead.

Bottle → Cup Weaning

Order to drop bottles

  1. Daytime bottles first — replace with cup at meals
  2. Mid-day/nap-adjacent bottles next
  3. Bedtime bottle last — but move milk itself to a cup earlier in the evening routine, then keep the bedtime step as book/song instead of a bottle

AAP target: start at 12 months, fully off the bottle by 18 months — this plan aims to finish by 14 months so it doesn't linger.

Why the bottle has to go

  • Prolonged bottle use is linked to tooth decay and overconsumption of milk
  • Never put her to bed with a bottle — pooled milk on teeth overnight is a major cavity risk
  • The longer the bottle stays, the more it competes with food and cup practice

Until now: water only with meals, capped at 8oz/day. At 12 months this reverses — she can have water between meals too, and the target climbs toward the 12-24 month range (see the Water & Milk Ref tab).

Water & Milk - Quick Reference

💧 Water & Milk — Quick Reference

Milk-anemia risk (12mo+)

More than 24oz/day of whole milk is the biggest single dietary risk after the switch — it displaces iron-rich food and can worsen iron absorption. Cap it even if she'd happily drink more.

Water intoxication (still relevant pre-12mo)

Infant kidneys can't clear a big water load until closer to 12 months. Stay inside the 11-month cap of 8oz/day until the water-rule flip. Never dilute formula to "stretch" it.

Vessel — open cup or straw, never hard-spout sippy

Hard sippy spouts block the tongue-elevation pattern needed for clear speech (t, d, n, l sounds) and can contribute to an open bite. Stick with an open cup or a soft silicone straw all the way through this transition.

During illness, still use Pedialyte

For diarrhea or vomiting, plain water still isn't the right rehydration tool at this age — an oral rehydration solution (Pedialyte) replaces electrolytes that plain water doesn't.

Never put her to bed with a bottle

Once milk moves into a bottle at all stages of this transition, keep it out of the crib. Pooled milk on erupting teeth overnight is a leading cause of early childhood cavities, and it undermines bottle weaning.

Sleep Training Protocol (Ferber)

😴 Sleep Training

Modified Ferber (Graduated Extinction) • Jan 2026

🎯 Goal: Independent Sleep (No Pacifier)

  • Problem: Waking 2x/night needing pacifier to go back to sleep
  • Solution: Teach self-soothing through Ferber method
  • Timeline: Typically 5-7 nights for significant improvement

⏱️ Check-In Intervals

Night First Wait Second Wait Subsequent
Night 1 3 min 5 min 10 min
Night 2 5 min 10 min 12 min
Night 3 10 min 12 min 15 min
Night 4+ 12 min 15 min 15 min

🌙 Bedtime Routine (Same Every Night)

  1. Bath
  2. Feed (end 20-30 min before crib)
  3. Pajamas / sleep sack (arms out)
  4. Book or song (buffer between feed & sleep)
  5. White noise on
  6. Put down awake, say goodnight, leave

✅ During Check-Ins

  1. Brief: Under 15 seconds
  2. Do NOT pick up
  3. Do NOT give pacifier
  4. Verbal only: "I love you, it's sleep time"
  5. One tummy pat, then leave

⏸️ If She Stops Crying Mid-Interval

Don't go in — she may be settling (the goal!)

If crying resumes: Reset timer for that interval

Fussing ≠ crying — wait through fussing

30+ sec quiet = real pause (reset if resumes)

5-10 sec pauses = keep timer running

👫 Who Does What

  • Dad: Nights 1-3 (babies protest less)
  • Mom: Rest! Earplugs / sleep elsewhere
  • After Night 3: Either parent can respond
  • Critical: BOTH must stay consistent — no caving!

📅 What to Expect

  • Night 1-2: Hardest. May cry 45-60 min. Normal!
  • Night 3-4: ⚠️ EXTINCTION BURST — often WORSE (she's fighting the change). Don't quit!
  • Night 5-7: Significant improvement, 10-15 min crying
  • Week 2+: New normal — fuss briefly, settles quickly

🌃 Night Wakings

Same intervals as bedtime

No pacifier, no picking up

No feeding — she can go 11+ hrs without food

Pre-6am wake? Treat as night waking (don't start day early)

🚨 If Checks Make It Worse

  • If she calms during check, screams harder when you leave → switch to extinction (no checks)
  • Trigger: 3x in a row = switch for that night
  • Or try verbal-only from doorway

🧠 Expert Consensus: Early Bedtime After Early Wake

  • Dr. Weissbluth: "Sleep begets sleep." Early bedtime (even 5:30pm) prevents cortisol spike. Best cure for early waking.

🔬 The Biology

Overtired = cortisol + adrenaline spike

Result = harder to fall asleep, more night wakes, early waking tomorrow

Early bedtime = catches her before spike → deeper sleep → often sleeps LATER

Myth: "Early bed = early wake" — FALSE for recovery days
